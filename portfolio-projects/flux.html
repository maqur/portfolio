FluxBeat

Overview

For our second year self proposed group project we designed a wireless power transmission device FluxBeat to power ventricular assist devices (VAD). Existing VAD requires a wire going through the body to power the device. This wire has several problems, most significant of which is Exit Site Infection (ESI). The product designed by our team aims to address this problem by removing the wire and attaching a battery to the VAD which can be charged wirelessly.

Outcome

We used magnetic induction to transfer power wirelessly. In the limited time that we had for the project we managed to built a protype that could charge an IPad pro with a distance of approximately 2-3 cms between the transmitter and receiver, even with some object in between. Checkout the videos of our early test and the presentation.

I was mainly involved in designing the circuit which involved an H-bridge for converting the DC current to AC as magnetic induction is only possible with AC, and building the prototype.
